Longevity

Telomere Extension Protocols

In the quest for extended human lifespan, few areas of research are as promising as telomere biology. Telomeres, the protective caps at the ends of our chromosomes, act as a molecular clock for cellular aging. As cells divide, these caps shorten, eventually leading to cellular senescence or death.

The Enzyme of Youth

Telomerase is an enzyme that can rebuild these protective caps, effectively reversing the cellular aging process. New clinical trials are exploring how controlled activation of telomerase can rejuvenate tissues and enhance systemic health in aging populations. At Carneta Health, we monitor these breakthroughs to provide insights into the future of biological maintenance.

Strategies involving high-intensity interval training (HIIT), NAD+ precursors, and specific antioxidants have shown potential in slowing telomere attrition. The future of healthcare lies not in treating disease, but in maintaining the integrity of our genetic clock.
