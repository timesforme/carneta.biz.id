Mastering Circadian Rhythms

Every cell in our body contains a biological clock governed by light exposure. When our internally generated rhythms are out of sync with our external environment, we experience metabolic disruption, sleep disorders, and cognitive decline.

The Master Clock

The Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN) in the brain is the master clock that regulates hormones like cortisol and melatonin. Early morning sunlight exposure (specifically blue light in the 480nm range) is the most powerful signal to the SCN to synchronize the body's rhythms for the day ahead, aiding in night-time melatonin production.

By protecting your eyes from artificial blue light after sunset and seeking natural light upon waking, you can reset your biological clock to its factory settings, ensuring peak vitality and recovery.
