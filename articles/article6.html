Intermittent Fasting & Autophagy

Autophagy, from the Greek meaning "self-eating," is the body's mechanism of adaptive cellular cleaning. By inducing metabolic stress through intermittent fasting, we can trigger the removal of damaged cell components.

Cellular Renewal

During a fast, insulin levels drop and glucagon rises, signaling the cells to begin recycling efficient proteins. This process is vital for preventing neurodegeneration and supporting overall longevity. At Carneta, we recommend starting with a 16:8 protocol to ease your body into metabolic flexibility.

The science shows that consistent periods of caloric restriction enhance mitochondrial efficiency, providing a cleaner and more stable energy source for the brain and body.
