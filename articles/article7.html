Gut Microbiome & Mental Health

The gut-brain axis is a bidirectional communication network between the central nervous system and the enteric nervous system. Your gut bacteria produce over 90% of your body's serotonin.

The Second Brain

The diversity of your gut microbiome directly influences your mood, anxiety levels, and cognitive function. A diet rich in fermented foods and diverse fiber profiles supports a healthy bacterial ecosystem, protecting against systemic inflammation and improving emotional resilience.

New research suggests that "psychobiotics"—probiotics that influence brain health—could be the next frontier in psychiatric care.
